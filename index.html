PROJECTS - FILM AND VIDEO

Over the years I have accumulated a vast and unique collection of film and video works.

Grá (2023)

Grá is an interactive art installation piece using elements of video, projection, and sculpture to explore themes of memory and trauma, specifically within the context of Northern Ireland’s “Troubles.” Edited with Adobe Premiere Pro.

Viewing link: https://youtu.be/XcXllw0P0-w

The Glistening Eastern Sea (2023)

The Glistening Eastern Sea is an experimental documentary film on my late grandfather. In this piece, I chose to explore the idea of having a conversation with someone who is physically absent. This idea presents itself in the long shots of nature as if nature’s ambiance speaks on behalf of the deceased individual. Edited with Adobe Premiere Pro.

Viewing link: https://youtu.be/x7wJntS__R0

How to Make a Bookshelf (2022)

How to Make a Bookshelf is an experimental short film in which the filmmaker uses an everyday item in a nonsensical and unconventional manner, to challenge traditional values and what we as a society deem unusual. Edited with Adobe Premiere Pro.

Viewing link: https://youtu.be/r_VgIHrIghw

Let It Roll (2023)

Let It Roll is an original short film based on a scene from my senior thesis screenplay. This scene focuses on the relationship between the main character and her mother. The film was a collaborative effort, thanks to my team of peers who worked seamlessly alongside me under a tight schedule. Edited with Adobe Premiere Pro.

Viewing link: https://youtu.be/riAB85yEPQ0

The Breakfast Club (2023)

The Breakfast Club is an experimental short film about the lake behind my high school, encouraging a discussion between the archival footage and the audience about the memories a space can hold. Edited with Adobe Premiere Pro.

Viewing link: https://youtu.be/xSTja_EHtZ0?si=dx4v9stBLRC3QV3X

A Portrait of Theo Haegele (2021)

A Portrait of Theo Haegele is a documentary film about my close friend, Theo Haegele, his passion for sculpture, and what it means to him philosophically and emotionally. Edited with Adobe Premiere Pro.

Viewing link: https://youtu.be/Ey3IvUQGG3U

The Inside of…. My Brain (2023)

The Inside of…. My Brain is a desktop documentary—utilizing modern Mac OS computer software as a base for collaging multiple screens or “tabs” to communicate emotions more often internalized than not.

Shut Up and Listen: The Kurt Cobain Special (2024)

I spearheaded the creation of a special segment for my college radio show at SUNY Purchase, honoring the 30th anniversary of Kurt Cobain’s passing, including a curated playlist.

Link to recorded segment: https://www.mixcloud.com/wpsr4ever/shut-up-and-listen-kurt-cobain-special/

Beatle-Media

I co-organized a curatorial exhibition and screening called “Beatle-Media” celebrating the Beatles through physical media (i.e. 16mm film, VHS, vinyl records, CDs).

Viewing link: https://youtube.com/shorts/IJPOwAFlrFY?feature=share

About Me

My name is Lilian Rolfs and I am a multimedia artist based in Pleasantville, New York. As a recent college graduate, I am currently seeking opportunities in screenwriting and film/video production. I earned a Bachelor of Arts in Screenwriting and an Associate in Film/Video Production at SUNY Purchase College. For my senior thesis, I wrote an 80-page feature-length screenplay titled, Let It Roll, in which I explored themes of nostalgia, familial ties, popular culture, and the everlasting impact of art, with a backdrop of 1980s Seattle. My goal as an artist and creator is to produce meaningful and resonant experiences for my audience. I am very interested in experimental approaches to filmmaking and storytelling to achieve that goal and incorporate the reinvention of traditional techniques.
